\title{Latex Tutorials 4 (Tables) Examples \\ Basic Tables}
\author{Andrew Roberts}
\maketitle

A very basic table. No lines. First column is left justified, the middle
is centered and the third is right.

\vspace{\baselineskip}
\begin{tabular}{ l c r }
	1 & 2 & 3 \\
	4 & 5 & 6 \\
	7 & 8 & 9 \\
\end{tabular}

\vspace{\baselineskip}

Same as above, but illustrating vertical lines.

\vspace{\baselineskip}
\begin{tabular}{ l | c || r |}
	1 & 2 & 3 \\
	4 & 5 & 6 \\
	7 & 8 & 9 \\
\end{tabular}

\vspace{\baselineskip}
The next table adds horizontal lines at the top and bottom.

\vspace{\baselineskip}
\begin{tabular}{ l | c || r | }
\hline
	1 & 2 & 3 \\
	4 & 5 & 6 \\
	7 & 8 & 9 \\
\hline
\end{tabular}

\vspace{\baselineskip}
Finally, more horizontal lines are added inbetween all rows. This table
has also been centered.

\begin{center}
	\begin{tabular}{ l | c || r | }
		\hline
		1 & 2 & 3 \\ \hline
		4 & 5 & 6 \\ \hline
		7 & 8 & 9 \\
	\hline
	\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\end{document}
